=====================================================================
METHOD: Baseline (No Smoothing)
=====================================================================

WHAT IT IS
----------
The unmodified ArchR IterativeLSI embedding, clustered and visualized
exactly as in the main binsize-comparison pipeline (5000bp tiles,
binarize=FALSE). No spatial information is used at this stage — this
is the reference point every smoothing method is judged against.

PARAMETERS
----------
- Input: IterativeLSI (ArchR default, 5000bp/binarizeFALSE config)
- Clustering: Seurat method, resolution = 0.8
- UMAP: nNeighbors = 30, minDist = 0.5, metric = cosine
- Reused directly from the on-disk ArchR project (not recomputed) so
  these numbers match analysis/binsize_comparison/archr_umap_cluster_comparison.pdf
  exactly.

INTERPRETATION
--------------
Spatial coherence (fraction of a spot's k=6 nearest physical neighbors
sharing its cluster label) ranges 0.55-0.78 at baseline — clusters are
already somewhat spatially contiguous (tissue biology is spatially
organized), but far from perfectly so, since clustering uses only
chromatin accessibility, with no spatial term. This is the expected
floor: any spatial-aware method should score higher than this on
coherence. Marker counts and log2FC here are the highest-confidence
numbers in the whole comparison, since no smoothing/blurring has been
applied to the underlying signal — a cluster differs from another
because the cells in it are molecularly different, not because nearby
spots were averaged together.

This method also has the lowest cluster counts (5-6) compared to
Alpha-Blend (7-9) and Iterative (9-11) — smoothing tends to fragment
the data into spatial sub-domains within what were previously single
clusters, since it accentuates small spatial gradients.
