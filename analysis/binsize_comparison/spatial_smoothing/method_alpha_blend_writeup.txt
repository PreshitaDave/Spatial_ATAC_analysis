=====================================================================
METHOD: Alpha-Blend (k=6, 1-hop, alpha=0.5)
=====================================================================

WHAT IT IS
----------
Each spot's LSI embedding is replaced by a 50/50 blend of its own
value and the mean of its 6 nearest *physical* (spatial) neighbors'
embeddings. A single smoothing pass. This is a mild, conservative
spatial-smoothing approach: every spot keeps half its own signal.

ALGORITHM
---------
1. Find each spot's 6 nearest neighbors by (x_spatial, y_spatial)
   (FNN::get.knn, which excludes the spot itself).
2. Build a row-normalized averaging matrix W (each row: 1/6 over the
   6 neighbors, 0 elsewhere).
3. New embedding Z' = 0.5 * Z + 0.5 * (W %*% Z)  — one pass, no
   iteration.
4. Re-cluster (Seurat, resolution 0.8) and re-run UMAP on Z'.

PARAMETERS
----------
- k = 6 spatial neighbors (self excluded from the averaging window,
  but retained via the 0.5 weight on the original value)
- alpha = 0.5 (blend weight)
- 1 smoothing pass (no iteration)
- Clustering: Seurat, resolution = 0.8 (same as baseline)

INTERPRETATION
--------------
Spatial coherence improves over baseline in every tissue (e.g.
lowseq_489: 0.706 -> 0.814; deepseq_488B: 0.569 -> 0.644), confirming
the blending does what it's designed to do — pull neighboring spots'
embeddings closer together, making cluster assignment more spatially
contiguous. It lands in the middle of the three methods on every
tissue: more spatially coherent than Baseline, less than Iterative.

Marker behavior is tissue-dependent: lowseq tissues gain markers
(more, smaller spatial sub-clusters get resolved as molecularly
distinct), while deepseq tissues lose some marker count relative to
baseline (e.g. deepseq_488B: 977 -> 737) without losing effect size
(mean log2FC stays ~0.59-0.64) — smoothing is spreading the same
signal over more clusters rather than degrading it.

Because it only blends 50% and does a single pass over only the
immediate 6 neighbors, this is the most conservative of the two
smoothing methods — a reasonable default when coherence gains are
wanted but distortion of the underlying molecular signal must be kept
minimal.
