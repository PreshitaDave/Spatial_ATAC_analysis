WHAT WAS MEASURED
-----------------
For each of 4 alignment x assignment conditions, a pseudobulk Xenium expression
profile was built per ATAC spot (mean log-normalized expression of Xenium cells
assigned to that spot) and correlated against the ArchR GeneScoreMatrix values,
across 377 shared genes. Two metrics: per-gene Pearson (across spots) and
per-spot Pearson (across genes). A multi-resolution sweep aggregated spots into
spatial grid bins of 0/25/50/100/200/400 um to test whether agreement improves
at coarser scales.

CONDITIONS TESTED
-----------------
affine_methodA:    Affine alignment + nearest-neighbor assignment (20um cutoff)
affine_methodB:    Affine alignment + Voronoi assignment (30um soft filter)
nonlinear_methodA: Nonlinear alignment + nearest-neighbor assignment
nonlinear_methodB: Nonlinear alignment + Voronoi assignment

CAN YOU TRUST THE NONLINEAR MAPPING?
-------------------------------------
Weakly yes, but with serious caveats:

1. CORRELATIONS ARE VERY LOW. Median per-gene Pearson of ~0.017 at native
   resolution is close to noise. Even at 200um bins it only reaches ~0.08-0.09.
   These values do not constitute strong validation of the nonlinear warp.

2. GENESCOREMATRIX IS NOT RNA EXPRESSION. The gene activity score is
   model-inferred from chromatin accessibility — the signal being correlated
   against Xenium is itself imputed, not measured. Low correlation could mean
   bad alignment, or lossy ATAC-to-gene imputation, or both. The two sources
   of error cannot be separated with this approach.

3. WHAT THE RESOLUTION SWEEP TELLS YOU. Correlation improving from 0.017 at
   native to ~0.09 at 200um bins is consistent with the alignment being
   approximately correct at coarse spatial scale but cell-level assignment
   uncertainty dominating at fine scale. The biology is real in aggregate;
   single-spot assignment is noisy.

4. NONLINEAR WINNING BY ~50% OVER AFFINE (0.017 vs 0.011) is a meaningful
   relative improvement even though absolute values are low. It suggests the
   nonlinear warp captures real tissue deformation. But it is not proof that
   the warp is accurate at single-cell resolution.

INDEPENDENT VALIDATION WITH scRNA-SEQ (NOT YET DONE)
------------------------------------------------------
Matched scRNA-seq data has been acquired for these tissues. Three options for
using it to validate the alignment independently of GeneScoreMatrix:

Option 1 — Label transfer validation (recommended first step):
  - Cluster scRNA-seq (Seurat/Scanpy), assign cell-type labels from RNA markers
  - Project labels onto spatial ATAC spots via the nonlinear + Method B alignment
  - Check if ArchR ATAC clusters correspond to spatially coherent scRNA cell types
  - This is fully independent of GeneScoreMatrix

Option 2 — Direct peak-to-gene correlation:
  - Use scRNA-seq gene expression as ground truth instead of Xenium pseudobulk
  - Drop-in replacement for xenium_expr in the notebook; loss function is reusable
  - Best if scRNA-seq has a larger gene panel than Xenium (377 shared genes)

Option 3 — Co-embedding / integration (strongest, most effort):
  - WNN or Seurat v4 bridge integration to jointly embed ATAC + RNA
  - Joint embedding quality is a measure of modality agreement, independent of
    spatial alignment entirely
  - Appropriate if scRNA-seq is from a different prep or different patients

RECOMMENDATION
--------------
Use nonlinear alignment + Method B (Voronoi) as the final alignment for all
downstream analyses. Validate further with scRNA-seq label transfer (Option 1)
before trusting fine-scale spatial assignments for CNV calling or cell-type
deconvolution.
