=====================================================================
SPATIAL SMOOTHING ANALYSIS SUMMARY
3-Method Comparison: Baseline vs Alpha-Blend vs Iterative
Tissues: lowseq_489, lowseq_488B, deepseq_488B, deepseq_489
Date: 2026-06-26
=====================================================================

METHODS
-------
Baseline:    No smoothing. Original LSI from IterativeLSI, resolution 0.8.
Alpha-Blend: k=6, 1-hop spatial kNN. Smoothed LSI = 0.5*self + 0.5*neighbor_avg.
             Distance cutoff = 3x median 1-NN distance (per-tissue adaptive).
Iterative:   k=8, 2-hop, self-inclusive averaging. No alpha blending.
             Two rounds of full neighbor averaging. Resolution 0.4 (lower to
             avoid cluster explosion).
Script: analysis/src/build_tissue/binsize_test_archr/compare_spatial_smoothing_methods.R

SPATIAL COHERENCE RESULTS (fraction of spatial neighbors sharing same cluster)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tissue          Baseline   Alpha-Blend   Iterative
lowseq_489      0.706      0.814         0.936
lowseq_488B     0.545      0.655         0.892
deepseq_488B    0.569      0.660         0.891
deepseq_489     0.775      0.776         0.930

Iterative wins on spatial contiguity in all 4 tissues by a large margin.

MARKER GENE COUNTS (GeneScoreMatrix, Wilcoxon, FDR<=0.05 & Log2FC>=0.5)

lowseq_489      2860       3661          3623        5 / 7 / 9

Mean Log2FC is nearly identical across all methods in all tissues (~0.62-0.63).
The drop in marker count for iterative in 488B tissues is likely due to
smaller/imbalanced clusters reducing Wilcoxon test power, not loss of biology.

CELL-TYPE IDENTITY COMPARISON (GeneScoreMatrix module scores)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Iterative finds new identity types not present in Alpha-Blend in all 4 tissues:
- lowseq_489:   HER2+ tumor, DNA-repair-deficient tumor
- lowseq_488B:  Endothelial, B cell
- deepseq_488B: Helper/Regulatory T cell, Epithelial/Tumor (general)
- deepseq_489:  DNA-repair-deficient tumor, B cell, Mast cell, Basal/myoepithelial,
                Epithelial/Tumor (general)

However, iterative also has MORE fragmentation (same identity recurring across
multiple clusters) than alpha-blend in 3/4 tissues. This means new identity
calls should be treated with caution — they may reflect smoothing-driven signal
mixing rather than genuinely distinct cell populations.

CAVEATS
-------
1. CIRCULAR VALIDATION: GeneScoreMatrix module scores were used both as the
   clustering input and the identity validation. This is not independent
   evidence that iterative finds more real biology.

2. MARKER COUNTS ARE NOT RNA: GeneScoreMatrix scores are imputed gene activity
   from chromatin accessibility — one inference layer removed from actual
   expression. "Marker genes" here are regions of differential accessibility
   near genes, not differentially expressed genes.

3. MARKER COUNT DROP IN ITERATIVE: Likely a statistical power artifact.
   More clusters with fewer cells each reduces Wilcoxon sensitivity.
   Mean Log2FC being unchanged confirms the signal strength is the same.

4. RESOLUTION ASYMMETRY: Iterative was run at resolution 0.4 vs 0.8 for the
   others. Even so, it produces 9-12 clusters vs 5-9 for the others.
   At resolution 0.8 iterative would likely over-fragment severely.

5. 2-HOP SMOOTHING TRADES MOLECULAR FOR SPATIAL RESOLUTION: By averaging
   neighbors' neighbors, iterative blurs fine-scale chromatin accessibility
   differences between adjacent cells. This is appropriate for spatial domain
   segmentation but may wash out subtle cell-type distinctions.

RECOMMENDED USE PER METHOD
---------------------------
Iterative:   Use for spatial domain segmentation — identifying tissue regions,
             tumor boundaries, stromal zones. Its high coherence (0.89-0.94)
             means clusters = contiguous spatial territories.

Alpha-Blend: Use for cell-type identification — which distinct cell types/
             identities are present. It gains some spatial coherence with less
             chromatin signal disturbance than iterative.

Baseline:    Use as the reference for CNV calling and any analysis where
             preserving the raw chromatin signal per cell is important.

NEXT STEPS (NOT YET DONE)
--------------------------
To biologically validate which smoothing method is best, the recommended path
is CNV profile consistency:
- Run alleloscope on cluster assignments from each method
- Compare intra-cluster CNV variance across methods
- The method with more internally homogeneous CNV profiles per cluster is
  more biologically meaningful
- This simultaneously validates the smoothing method AND advances CNV calling

H&E images are not available for these tissues, so spatial histology
co-localization is not feasible as a validation approach.
