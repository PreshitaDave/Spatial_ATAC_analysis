=====================================================================
OVERALL INTERPRETATION
=====================================================================
Iterative finds new identity types in 4/4 tissues.
Iterative has MORE clusters sharing a repeated identity (fragmentation) than
Alpha-Blend in 3/4 tissues (Iterative fragmented clusters: 1,2,3,4; Alpha-Blend: 1,1,2,3)

Taken together with the spatial-coherence result (Iterative wins every
tissue, 0.89-0.93 vs 0.65-0.81): Iterative is NOT simply finding more real
cell types end-to-end. It is mostly subdividing biology that Alpha-Blend
already resolves into more spatially homogeneous pieces, with the same
identity recurring across multiple Iterative clusters in most tissues.
Where Iterative DOES surface genuinely new identity calls, treat them with
caution: GeneScoreMatrix module-score confidence gaps are modest in both
methods (means below ~0.5 in most tissues), so a low-confidence "new"
identity may reflect smoothing-driven signal mixing rather than a real
distinct cell population. Recommendation: use Iterative for spatial-domain
segmentation (its intended purpose -- 2-hop smoothing trades molecular
resolution for spatial contiguity), and Alpha-Blend (or Baseline) when the
question is which distinct cell types/identities are present, since it
disturbs the underlying chromatin-accessibility signal the least while
still gaining some spatial coherence.
